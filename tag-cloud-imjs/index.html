    aap-1

    Species:
    Caenorhabditis elegans
    Sequence:
    Y110A7A.10
    Public name:
    aap-1
    Other names:
    Type:
    protein coding
    Gene class:
    aap
    Operon:
    CEOP1906
    Status:
    Live
    Cloned By:
    WormBase ID:
    WBGene00000001
    aap-1 encodes the C. elegans ortholog of the phosphoinositide 3-kinase (PI3K) p50/p55 adaptor/regulatory subunit; AAP-1 negatively regulates lifespan and dauer development, and likely functions as the sole adaptor subunit for the AGE-1/p110 PI3K catalytic subunit to which it binds in vitro; although AAP-1 potentiates insulin-like signaling, it is not absolutely required for insulin-like signaling under most conditions.
    Date last updated: 02 Mar 2006
    Curator confirmed: Kimberly Van Auken; Carol Bastiani
    Paper evidence: Wolkow, Munoz, Riddle, & Ruvkun, 2002; Munoz & Riddle, 2003
    evidence
    Details
    Sequence features:
    AAP-1 contains the hallmarks of a PI3K p50/p55 adaptor subunit: two SH2 domains flanking a short inter-SH2 domain. These two SH2 domains are most cloesly related to those of mammalian PI3K p85 and p55.
    Date last updated: 02 Mar 2006
    Curator confirmed: Kimberly Van Auken
    Paper evidence: Wolkow, Munoz, Riddle, & Ruvkun, 2002
    evidence
    Biological process:
    aap-1(m889) animals are long-lived, and when grown at 27 degrees C, they arrest development as dauer larvae.
    Date last updated: 02 Mar 2006
    Curator confirmed: Kimberly Van Auken
    Paper evidence: Wolkow, Munoz, Riddle, & Ruvkun, 2002
    evidence
    Functional physical interaction:
    Bacterially expressed AAP-1 co-precipitates an amino-terminal fragment (1-286) of AGE-1, which contains the AGE-1 intersubunit interaction domain.
    Date last updated: 02 Mar 2006
    Curator confirmed: Kimberly Van Auken
    Paper evidence: Wolkow, Munoz, Riddle, & Ruvkun, 2002
    evidence
